Our Research


Drinking frequency among different age groups


Analyze of the change in drinking frequency across age showed that the 21-29 group is the only significantly growing market segment. Specifically, 47% of the 20s express drinking more wine than a couple years ago.

Since 21 is the legal drinking age in the U.S., it makes sense that 21-29 years old are new to the wine world, learning and exploring. In contrast, other age groups drinking frequencies are relatively stable. Thus we decided to target people in their 20s believing that they have the strongest need to explore wines but with limited wine knowledge.

Understand affordable price & wine rating


Q: What does affordability mean for wine purchasers?

We found that wine cheaper than 20 dollars are the most popular among wine drinkers. In fact, most people never buy wines over 20 dollars. Thus, we filtered our wine dataset under 20 dollars, believing it is a standard for affordability in this project.

Q: What does affordability mean for wine purchasers?

Not necessarily. The data showed that the most expensive wine is not rated very high, while higher price does not necessarily guarantee better wines. In other words, affordable wines can also be good wines.
